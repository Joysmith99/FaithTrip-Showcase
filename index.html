Abstract

Trip planning aims to create personalized travel itineraries that match tourists’ preferences. Traditional heuristic and statistical approaches typically plan trips based on popularity, queue times, or transition probabilities among points of interest (POIs). Recent learning-based approaches improve accuracy but struggle to generate faithful trajectories that align with tourists’ intents, where the recommended source and destination sometimes differ from the user-specified ones. To address this, some studies generate only the intermediate sub-trajectory, which is then combined with the user-specified source and destination. Others add an auxiliary loss term to increase the penalty for recommending mismatched sources and destinations. However, the former often leads to trajectory distortion, while the latter demands a careful trade-off between accuracy and faithfulness and still falls short of fully resolving the issue. In this study, we propose \M, a generative trip planning approach that explicitly aligns generated trajectories with tourists’ intents through training-free diffusion guidance. Compared to eleven advanced baselines on four real-world public datasets, \M achieves 100\% faithfulness, improves Pairs-F1 score by 10.1\%, and reduces distance deviation by 36.2\%, significantly enhancing the reliability of the trip planning system.
